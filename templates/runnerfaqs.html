Runner FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to the most common runner questions.

How do I accept an errand?

When a new job appears in your dashboard, tap Accept within the time limit. You can also enable Auto‑Accept in Settings to automatically take eligible jobs.

How do I get paid?

The client pays when you deliver. If the errand required you to purchase goods, you will be reimbursed the item cost plus your delivery fee. If the client refuses to pay, report it immediately – ErrandGo will pay you within 24 hours.

Can I cancel an accepted job?

Yes, but only before you mark yourself as “On the way”. Frequent cancellations may lower your rating and affect job offers.

What if the sender is unreachable?

Use the in‑app chat. If they don’t respond within a reasonable time, tap “Client Not Responding” in the errand screen and wait for further instructions. Do not leave the package unattended.

Should I buy items with my own money?

Yes, if the errand requires purchasing goods. Keep the receipt! You’ll get your money back (item cost + delivery fee) when you hand over the items.

Do I need to share my location?

It’s recommended. If the client requests it, you must enable Location Services so they can track the delivery in real time. You can manage this in your phone’s settings.

Why do I need a high rating?

Runners with high ratings get more job offers and can earn free errands (e.g., 5 consecutive 5‑star ratings = 3 free errands).

How do I update my vehicle information?

Go to Settings → Personal & Vehicle Info. Vehicle type and plate can only be changed by contacting support if needed.

What should I do if I feel unsafe?

Your safety comes first. If you feel threatened, leave the area and immediately call the emergency number in the Help section. Report the incident to ErrandGo afterwards.
