1. Introduction

Welcome to ErrandGo Runner Program

This Runner Agreement ("Agreement") governs your relationship with ErrandGo as an independent contractor providing errand services through our platform. By registering as a Runner, you agree to be bound by these terms.

Client

Posts errand & pays you

You (Runner)

Completes errand & gets paid

ErrandGo

Matches & ensures payment

Key Points for Runners

• You are an independent contractor, not an employee
• You keep 90% of earnings, pay 10% platform fee weekly
• ErrandGo compensates unpaid fees within 24 hours
• You must maintain package integrity and communicate delays

2. Independent Contractor Status

You Are an Independent Contractor

As a Runner, you are an independent contractor. This means:

Set Your Own Hours

You decide when and how much to work

Provide Your Own Tools

You use your own vehicle, phone, and equipment

Handle Your Own Taxes

You are responsible for your own tax payments

Maintain Your Insurance

You need your own liability insurance

What This Agreement Means

No Employment Benefits

No health insurance, paid leave, or retirement benefits from ErrandGo

No Guaranteed Work

ErrandGo does not guarantee minimum work hours or earnings

Control Your Work

You decide which errands to accept and how to complete them

Multiple Platforms

You may work for other platforms or businesses simultaneously

Tax Responsibility

As an independent contractor, you are responsible for paying your own taxes, including income tax and self-employment tax. Keep records of all your earnings and expenses. Consider consulting with a tax professional.

8. Package Integrity & Damages

Package Integrity Responsibility

As a Runner, you are responsible for maintaining the integrity of all packages and items entrusted to you. This includes:

Visual Inspection

Inspect packages upon receipt and note any existing damage

Photo Documentation

Take photos of packages before and after transport

Proper Handling

Handle all items with care using appropriate techniques

Temperature Control

Maintain required temperatures for perishable items

Damage Liability Rules

Runner is Liable

PAY

• Damage caused by improper handling
• Loss due to negligence
• Items left unattended in unsafe locations
• Failure to follow specific handling instructions

Runner is NOT Liable

NO PAY

• Pre-existing damage (with photo proof)
• Damage caused by improper packaging
• Acts of God (weather, accidents)
• Client provides wrong delivery information

Damage Reporting Procedure

Step 1: Document Immediately

Take clear photos of any damage as soon as discovered

Immediately upon discovery

Step 2: Notify Client

Inform client immediately via ErrandGo chat with photos

Within 15 minutes

Step 3: Report to ErrandGo

File official damage report through the app with all evidence

Within 1 hour
